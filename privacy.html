💾 Your data saves automatically

The app saves everything — accounts, balances, imported transactions, budgets, and snapshots — to your browser's local storage. It persists across refreshes and restarts, even without signing in. Your browser processes CSV files locally and nothing leaves your device unless you choose to sync. You can export your transactions and budget history as a CSV anytime from the Spending and Budget tabs, respectively.

🔁 Sign in to sync across devices

Sign in with Google and the app backs up your full data — accounts, transactions, budgets, categories, and snapshots — directly to your Google account via Firebase. Google enforces access at the database level so only you can read it. Sign in on any device to restore everything instantly.

🗂 Community categorization rules

Each time you open the import screen, the app downloads community-rules.json from our Firebase hosting once per session. This file contains keyword→category mappings the app uses to auto-categorize your transactions. The app sends no transaction data, descriptions, or personal information — it only downloads the rules file. The file lives publicly on GitHub so anyone can audit it. Missing a merchant? Suggest a pattern ↗ — no account required. You can block this download using any privacy extension and the app will still work fine.

📊 Analytics — what we collect

When you sign in with Google, Firebase Analytics automatically collects three event types: first_visit (your first time opening the app), session_start (each visit), and page_view (each navigation). Each event includes browser language, page URL, screen resolution, and a randomly generated anonymous client ID. The app never sends names, emails, financial figures, or account data. You can stop this entirely using any privacy extension.

Frequently asked questions

Can the developer see my financial data?+

No — and this isn't just a policy. It's how the app is built. By default, the app saves your data to your browser on your device. The developer built no mechanism to transmit it anywhere and runs no server to receive it. If you choose to sign in with Google, your data syncs to your personal Google account via Firebase — Google holds it, not us, and only you can read it. Either way, the developer has no way to see your financial data.

Could a hacker access my financial data?+

No — neither the app nor the developer holds your financial data, so there's nothing to steal. That said, here are the three things someone could theoretically attack, and how each is protected:

  • The app files — hosted on Firebase. Deploying any change requires the developer's Google account credentials plus their physical machine, and that Google account uses two-factor authentication (a password plus a second confirmation, like a phone prompt). An attacker would need both to make any change.
  • The source code — stored on GitHub, also protected with two-factor authentication. Because the source is public, anyone can verify exactly what the app does and spot anything unexpected.
  • Your synced data — only relevant if you've signed in with Google. Firebase security rules enforce that your data is accessible only with your own Google credentials. An attacker would need to compromise your personal Google account, not the developer's.

None of these paths expose your financial data — the app and the developer simply don't hold it.

What if this app shuts down?+

Two things could happen: the developer could stop updating the app, or take it offline entirely. If the app goes offline, you'd see a 'page not found' message when you try to open it — but your data isn't affected either way. If you haven't signed in, the app already stores everything on your device and it stays there. If you have signed in with Google, your data lives in your personal Google account, which you own and control directly with Google — independently of this app. Either way, export your transactions from the Spending tab and your budget history from the Budget tab anytime as a backup. Your data outlives the app.

Is my CSV safe after I import it?+

Yes. Your browser translates your CSV file into spending data entirely on your device — think of it like opening a document in Word, where the file never leaves your computer. The app saves your transaction records to your device only. The developer never receives the file or its contents. If you've signed in with Google, the app also syncs those transaction records to your personal Google account. Your original CSV file stays on your computer throughout.

Could the developer share my data with governments or law enforcement?+

No — and this is technical, not just policy. If you haven't signed in, the app stores your data only on your device and the developer receives none of it, so there's nothing to hand over. If you have signed in with Google, your data lives in your personal Google account — any legal request would need to go to Google directly, not to the developer of this app. Either way, there's nothing for the developer to hand over.

Does this app use cookies?+

Firebase Analytics — a Google service the developer uses to understand how many people use the app — activates only when you sign in. It may set cookies through Google's infrastructure that carry session-level data (visits, page views). These cookies contain no financial data and go to Google, not to the developer directly. You can block them using any privacy extension and the app will still work fine. You can review exactly what Firebase Analytics collects at firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

What if my device has spyware on it?+

Spyware is malicious software that secretly records what happens on your device — it could capture anything visible on your screen or stored locally, including data this app saves to your device. This risk applies to all software that runs locally, not just this app. The developer can only control what's in the app's code — your device is yours to protect. Keeping your operating system and browser updated is the single most effective step you can take.
