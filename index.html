Who built this?

One person — Nick Garofalo. I tracked my money in Python and Google Sheets for years. It worked, but I knew the analysis could go deeper — a clear picture of what spending looks like now, and what it might look like through the big changes: a move, a house, a car, a wedding, a kid. Every app that offered that wanted my bank login. So I built this instead. It's open source — read the code yourself ↗

Common questions.

Does it work with my bank?

If your bank lets you download transactions as a CSV — and nearly all of them do — you're good. Chase, Ally, Bank of America, Amex, USAA, TD, RBC, Barclays, Monzo, and most credit unions all work out of the box. Look for a Download or Export button in your transaction history.

Is this a Mint replacement?

For spending, budgeting, and net worth tracking, yes. The main difference: Mint connected directly to your bank and synced automatically. This app doesn't — you import CSVs for spending and enter account balances manually. That's a deliberate trade-off. You update on your schedule, your data stays on your device, and there's nothing to breach.

Where is my data stored?

In your browser's local storage by default — on your device, not a server. Try it — close the tab, come back, it's all still there. Signing in adds optional encrypted sync (see Privacy above for how the passphrase works) and can be undone at any time. Full data flow →
